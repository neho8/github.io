Start Learning Linux

Date: 22.09.2023 + Video below


Linux For Everyone

Whether you're an IT professional or curious to explore the world of hacking, learning and mastering the Linux operation system is crucial.

For IT professionals, learning Linux is central for various advanced job roles, including DevOps, cloud, system, and network administration, making it a key asset for a successful IT career.

For cyber enthusiasts - Linux is an excellent choice due to its restrictions-free environments and other ideal advantages, unlike Windows and macOS limitations due to their closed systems and proprietary software.


What Is Linux?

In 1991, Linus Torvalds started the Linux kernel. What turns it into an operating system are the extras added to it, like a package manager, desktop environment, shell, bootloader, and more.

Linux's open-source nature has led to countless customizations, resulting in a wide range of distributions. These distributions, numbering in the hundreds, if not thousands, serve specific purposes or easily cater to the creative interests of users.

Notable distributions include Ubuntu (widely used), Kali Linux (gathers a great selection of hacking tools), Debian (classy), Arch Linux (preferred by experts), and Red Hat Enterprise Linux (commercial and costly).


Linux Terminal

To unleash Linux's full power, you should dive into the command line or terminal. Even though there's a graphical user interface (GUI) available, professionals and hackers prefer the terminal for its speed, efficiency, and versatility.

To learn your first Linux commands and to be able to operate through the terminal, I recommend that you watch the entirety of the Linux for Hackers video series by YouTuber NetworkChuck.

While watching the video series, I strongly suggest practicing the commands using a Linux virtual machine with the "Parrot" distribution, which is the same distribution featured in the videos. In the next article - Introduction to Virtualization, I will clarify Virtualization and explain how to set up your own virtual machine.

After you have finished watching all the videos in the series, you can start practicing the Linux commands in all kinds of exercises that you find on the Internet. Here is one great practice you can do. Don't worry if in the middle of the exercise you have to learn a new command that you didn't know, that's the beauty of it! keep learning and developing.


In conclusion

Discovering Linux is a must for IT pros and budding hackers alike! We introduce Linux, its diverse flavors, and the thrill of command-line magic. Explore the journey with fun tutorials and practical exercises - whether you're a pro or just getting started, Linux has something exciting for everyone!
